Inubaran

Ingredients

  • Banana pith (ubad), finely sliced
  • Chicken, bite-size pieces
  • Garlic, onion, ginger
  • Coconut milk (thin and thick)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Oil for sautéing
  • Calamansi or vinegar (optional, to brighten)

Instructions (step by step)

  1. Prep ubad: Remove tough outer layers of banana pith; slice the tender core thinly. Rinse and drain.
  2. Sauté aromatics: In a pot, heat oil. Sauté garlic, onion, and ginger until fragrant.
  3. Brown chicken: Add chicken pieces and cook until lightly browned.
  4. Simmer in thin coconut milk: Pour in thin coconut milk (or diluted coconut milk). Season with salt and pepper. Simmer 10–15 minutes.
  5. Add ubad: Stir in sliced banana pith. Cook 5–8 minutes until tender but still crisp.
  6. Finish with thick coconut milk: Add thick coconut milk; simmer gently (do not boil hard) until the sauce is creamy and chicken is cooked through.
  7. Balance: Adjust seasoning. Add a splash of calamansi or vinegar if you prefer a bright finish.
  8. Serve: Ladle into bowls and enjoy with rice.

History

Inubaran showcases Bukidnon ingenuity—using banana pith with coconut milk—to create a nourishing, distinctly local dish.
