Binaki

Ingredients

  • Fresh corn kernels (or finely ground corn)
  • Coconut milk
  • Sugar
  • Pinch of salt
  • Corn husks (cleaned) for wrapping

Instructions (step by step)

  1. Prep husks: Rinse and pat dry corn husks. If stiff, briefly steam or soak to soften.
  2. Make batter: In a bowl, combine corn, coconut milk, sugar, and a pinch of salt. Mix until thick but scoopable.
  3. Wrap: Lay two husks overlapping. Spoon 2–3 tablespoons of batter in the center and fold sides in, then fold ends to seal.
  4. Tie (optional): Use thin strips of husk to tie each parcel gently.
  5. Steam: Arrange wrapped binaki in a steamer over simmering water. Steam 25–35 minutes until set and slightly springy.
  6. Cool: Let rest 5 minutes before serving so the texture firms up.
  7. Serve: Enjoy warm, straight from the husk, or with grated coconut.

History

Binaki is a Bukidnon harvest delicacy—steamed corn pudding wrapped in husks—shared to celebrate abundance and community.
