Linupak

Ingredients

  • Cassava (or ripe saba banana)
  • Grated coconut
  • Sugar (adjust to taste)
  • Butter or coconut oil (optional, for richness)
  • Pinch of salt
  • Bananaleaf for lining/serving (optional)

Instructions (step by step)

  1. Prep cassava: Peel, chunk, and rinse. Boil in salted water until tender; drain well. If using saba, steam until soft.
  2. Mash: While warm, pound or mash cassava/banana in a large bowl until smooth.
  3. Mix: Add sugar, grated coconut, and a pinch of salt. Mix until combined. Add butter or coconut oil if you like it richer.
  4. Shape: With damp hands, form logs or patties. You can press into a lined mold for neat portions.
  5. Serve: Place on banana leaves, sprinkle extra grated coconut on top, and serve slightly warm.
  6. Store: Keep leftovers covered; rewarm gently to restore softness.

History

Linupak is a communal Bukidnon delicacy—pounded root crops shared in gatherings—symbolizing unity and shared labor.
